Profile

Scientific Name- Pavo cristatus

Population- 10,000 to 20,000

Weight- 4 to 6 kg(approx.)

Length- 100 to 120cm

Habitats- Forests

Food- Fruits, Berries, Ants, Grains and Plants

Status- Endangered

Facts

Peahens build their nests on the ground, in a shallow hole padded with grass. They usually make their nests in areas that are sheltered by bushes, shrubs, or other vegetation, to keep the eggs safe from predators. A peacock’s tail feathers can reach up to six feet long and make up about 60 percent of its body length. Despite these odd proportions, the bird flies just fine, if not very far.

Importance

Peacock is the national bird of India and hence very important for the Indians. It is really a rare and lovely sight for the beauty-loving people. So the peacock has rightly been chosen as the national bird of our country. Its rhythm of gait, it’s a keenness to the green vegetation, its keen response to the changing season-all have drawn. It is closer to the poet than any other bird. The Indian sub-continent is the native place of the peacock and they are found in different other parts of Asia. The male peacock is good looking but the female peacock is very ugly to look. It has two rough legs and a long beak. The most beautiful part of a male peacock is its long tail. One of the most beautiful sights in the world of birds is that of a peacock displaying the beauty of its glittering tail features.
